### Introduction

I lived in Nagoya for about two years and had the chance to travel throughout Japan, visiting many incredible places along the way. This guide is a list of destinations I’ve personally explored, along with highlights of things to do in each. Think of this as an overview of the _Nagoya Nostalgia_ series I mentioned in my previous post.

In this post, I have recommendations for **Hokkaido, Tokyo, Nagoya, Kyoto, Osaka, Nara, Kobe, Hiroshima, and Okinawa**. The sections below provide a very brief overview of notable spots and activities, and each entry includes a link for further information.

I've been to all these places unless noted otherwise, and plan to share more detailed stories and insights in future posts. Since everything here is based on my own experiences, I also encourage you to do additional research to find what best fits your interests and travel style.

At the end of the post, I’ve included a few extra tips for traveling in Japan.

### Hokkaido

- **<a href="https://www.japan-guide.com/e/e5300.html" target="_blank" style="color: #b58900; text-decoration: underline;">Sapporo Beer Museum:</a>**
  Discover the rich history of Japanese beer, enjoy curated tastings, and experience local restaurants along the way!
- **<a href="https://www.japan-guide.com/e/e5309.html/" target="_blank" style="color: #b58900; text-decoration: underline;">Mt. Moiwa Ropeway:</a>**
  Take a cable car (or hike) up Mt. Moiwa and get a spectacular view of Sapporo from above.
- **<a href="https://www.japan-guide.com/e/e5307.html" target="_blank" style="color: #b58900; text-decoration: underline;">Shiroi Koibito Park:</a>** Visit the sweets factory where Japan's famous _Shiroi Koibito_ (White Lover) cookies are made.

- **<a href="https://www.japan-guide.com/e/e6700.html" target="_blank" style="color: #b58900; text-decoration: underline;">Otaru:</a>**
  A quaint canal-side city brimming with charming shops and captivating museums.

### Tokyo

- **<a href="https://www.japan-guide.com/e/e3007.html" target="_blank" style="color: #b58900; text-decoration: underline;">Shibuya:</a>**
  Shopping and entertainment district with the famous Shibuya crossing. Close to Shinjuku and Akihabara.

- **<a href="https://www.japan-guide.com/e/e3011.html" target="_blank" style="color: #b58900; text-decoration: underline;">Shinjuku:</a>**
  Another large shopping and entertainment district, famous for Japanese night life. Close to Shibuya and Akihabara.

- **<a href="https://www.japan-guide.com/e/e3003.html" target="_blank" style="color: #b58900; text-decoration: underline;">Akihabara:</a>**
  Paradise for anime and video game lovers. Check out Radio Kaikan! Close to Shinjuku and Shibuya.

- **<a href="https://www.japan-guide.com/e/e3004.html" target="_blank" style="color: #b58900; text-decoration: underline;">Asakusa:</a>**
  Historic part of Tokyo, famous for very popular temples such as Sensoji.

- **<a href="https://www.japan-guide.com/e/e3008.html" target="_blank" style="color: #b58900; text-decoration: underline;">Odaiba:</a>**
  Popular man-made island with large shopping centers and The Statue of Liberty!

- **<a href="https://www.ichigaya-fc.com/shop_guide_e_f.html" target="_blank" style="color: #b58900; text-decoration: underline;">Ichigaya Fishing Center:</a>** Catch-and-release fishing of giant carp near Ichigaya Station.

- **<a href="https://www.japan-guide.com/e/e3080.html" target="_blank" style="color: #b58900; text-decoration: underline;">Kichijoji:</a>**
  A hidden gem located slightly outside Tokyo. I recommend visiting Inokashira Park, where you can take in the natural beauty while row boating or pedaling a swan boat!

### Nagoya

- **<a href="https://www.nagoya-info.jp/en/spot/detail/19/" target="_blank" style="color: #b58900; text-decoration: underline;">Osu Shopping Center:</a>** Large shopping district that spans several streets throughout.

- **<a href="https://www.japan-guide.com/e/e3301.html" target="_blank" style="color: #b58900; text-decoration: underline;">Atsuta Jingu Shrine:</a>** One of Japan's most important shrines. Houses one of the three imperial regalia, the sacred sword Kusanagi.

- **<a href="https://www.nagoya-info.jp/en/spot/detail/33/" target="_blank" style="color: #b58900; text-decoration: underline;">Shiratori Garden:</a>** Tranquil Japanese garden with a traditional tea house.

- **<a href="https://www.nagoya-info.jp/en/spot/detail/91/" target="_blank" style="color: #b58900; text-decoration: underline;">Hisaya-Odori Park:</a>** Central Nagoya park with shopping and Mirai TV tower.

- **<a href="https://www.japan-guide.com/e/e3307.html" target="_blank" style="color: #b58900; text-decoration: underline;">Port of Nagoya Public Aquarium:</a>**
  On of Japan's largest aquariums near the port of Nagoya!

- **<a href="https://www.japan-guide.com/e/e3300.html" target="_blank" style="color: #b58900; text-decoration: underline;">Nagoya Castle:</a>** Famous Japanese castle built in 1612 (rebuilt in 1959).

Many people believe Nagoya is boring. As someone who has lived there, I don't think Nagoya is boring. I absolutely love Nagoya. Check out <a href="https://www.nagoyaisnotboring.com" target="_blank" style="color: #b58900; text-decoration: underline;">**nagoyaisnotboring.com**</a>!

### Kobe

- <a href="https://www.japan.travel/en/spot/1039/" target="_blank" style="color: #b58900; text-decoration: underline;">**Kobe Port Tower:**</a> An iconic waterfront landmark offering panoramic views of Kobe’s skyline and harbor.

- <a href="https://www.japan-guide.com/e/e3563.html" target="_blank" style="color: #b58900; text-decoration: underline;">**Kobe Ropeway and Herb Gardens:**</a> A scenic cable car ride leading to lush gardens filled with seasonal flowers, herbs, and sweeping mountain views.

- <a href="https://www.japan-guide.com/e/e3553.html" target="_blank" style="color: #b58900; text-decoration: underline;">**Kobe Harborland:**</a> A lively shopping and entertainment district by the sea, featuring restaurants, boutiques, and waterfront strolls.

- <a href="https://www.japan-guide.com/e/e3558.html" target="_blank" style="color: #b58900; text-decoration: underline;">**Arima Onsen:**</a> One of Japan’s oldest hot spring towns, known for its relaxing mineral-rich baths and traditional atmosphere.

- <a href="https://www.japan-guide.com/e/e3560.html" target="_blank" style="color: #b58900; text-decoration: underline;">**Sorakuen Garden**:</a> A serene Japanese landscape garden in central Kobe, featuring traditional ponds, teahouses, and seasonal flowers.

- <a href="https://www.japan-guide.com/e/e3501.html" target="_blank" style="color: #b58900; text-decoration: underline;">**Himeji Castle**:</a> A bit outside Kobe, famed for its elegant white exterior and well-preserved feudal architecture.

### Tips for transportation

Trains are the most convenient way to get around Japan. Here are some apps that can make riding both bullet trains and local subways/trains easier:

- **smartEx:** This app lets you buy bullet train tickets and scan a QR code directly at the gate. It can be a bit finicky, but once it works, it saves a lot of hassle. Be persistent when logging in. For guaranteed seating, buy reserved seat tickets. Otherwise, you may have to stand, as trains can get busy. Without this app, you might need two tickets per person to ride the bullet train, which is doable but slightly tricky. Here’s a helpful guide: <a href="https://www.jrailpass.com/blog/shinkansen-tickets" target="_blank" style="color: #b58900; text-decoration: underline;">Shinkansen Tickets: How to Buy and Use</a>

- **Welcome Suica Mobile:** Suica is a rechargeable transit card that works for trains, subways, and buses. This app is the digital version, allowing you to scan your phone at ticket gates. I personally used a physical card, but this app is convenient if you prefer not to buy individual tickets. _Note : I think you can only load money on this app after you get to Japan._

### Tips for booking accommodations

I usually book hotels through Agoda, which often has great deals, especially if you plan early. In Japan, you can choose shared dormitories (your own bunk bed in a room with ~10 people) for a budget-friendly option, or private rooms at reasonable prices if traveling with a friend.

### General tips

I also recommend checking apps like Klook or Agoda for local activities. They often list museums and experiences that aren’t widely advertised.

If you’re visiting very popular spots, especially in Tokyo, make reservations at least two weeks in advance to avoid disappointment.
